## 1. Brodmann Area - Gyrus/Sulcus Mapping Table

| Brodmann Area | Gyrus/Sulcus | Function | Emotion Relevance |
|--------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| BA25 (sgACC) | Subgenual anterior cingulate cortex (below genu of corpus callosum) | Visceromotor control, autonomic regulation | **Sadness center**: overactive in treatment-resistant depression; DBS target (Mayberg 2005). Rich in serotonin transporters. Governor for amygdala, hypothalamus, brainstem |
| BA24 (dACC) | Dorsal anterior cingulate gyrus (supragenual) | Conflict monitoring, error detection, pain processing | Part of salience network with anterior insula. Skeletomotor control, cognitive-motor division of cingulate |
| BA32 (pACC) | Pregenual/paracingulate gyrus | Self-referential processing, social cognition | Perigenual affective division. Co-activated with both PAG and hypothalamus during emotion (Kober 2008) |
| BA11/47 (OFC) | Orbitofrontal cortex (gyrus rectus, orbital gyri) | Reward/punishment representation, value-based decisions | Medial OFC = reward; lateral OFC = non-reward/punishment (Rolls 2020). Connected to amygdala via uncinate fasciculus |
| BA46/9 (dlPFC) | Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (middle frontal gyrus, lateral superior frontal gyrus) | Working memory, executive control, cognitive flexibility | Top-down emotion regulation via inhibitory connections to amygdala and ACC (Ochsner & Gross 2005) |
| BA10 (FPC) | Frontopolar cortex (anterior prefrontal cortex) | Prospective memory, counterfactual reasoning, metacognition | Higher-order integration of emotional and cognitive information. Pessimistic future-thinking in depression |
| BA13/14/16 (anterior insula) | Anterior insular cortex (agranular insula) | Interoception, awareness, salience detection | Hub of salience network (Craig 2009). Von Economo neurons for emotional awareness. Hyperactive in anxiety disorders (Etkin & Wager 2007) |
| BA (posterior insula) | Posterior insular cortex (granular insula) | Somatosensory integration, pain perception | Receives polymodal vestibular, interoceptive, proprioceptive inputs. Relays to anterior insula for emotional awareness |
| BA38 | Temporal pole (anterior temporal lobe) | Social cognition, semantic memory, face recognition | Convergence zone binding sensory concepts with emotional significance. Right=emotion-social memory; Left=semantic labels (Olson 2007) |
| BA37 (FFA) | Fusiform gyrus (fusiform face area, mid-fusiform) | Face perception, visual object recognition | Face-selective activation (Kanwisher 1997). Differentially activated by emotional faces; enhanced by fearful expressions |
| BA22 (STS) | Superior temporal sulcus | Biological motion, social perception, theory of mind | Processing social signals: gaze direction, facial expressions, body language. Integration of audiovisual emotional cues |
| BA23/31 (PCC) | Posterior cingulate cortex / precuneus | Self-referential processing, autobiographical memory | Core node of default mode network. Active during emotion-related self-reflection and internal mentation |
| BA28/34 | Entorhinal cortex (parahippocampal gyrus, anterior) | Memory gateway to hippocampus | Part of Papez circuit. Interface between cortical association areas and hippocampal memory system |
| BA35/36 | Perirhinal/parahippocampal cortex | Object recognition, contextual memory | Emotional context encoding. Contextual fear conditioning pathways |

## 2. White Matter Pathways

| Pathway | Origin | Termination | Function | Key Papers |
|---------|--------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Papez circuit** | Hippocampus (subiculum) | Hippocampus (circular: subiculum -> fornix -> mammillary bodies -> mammillothalamic tract -> anterior thalamus -> cingulum -> entorhinal cortex -> hippocampus) | Memory and emotion circuit. Theta rhythm propagation | Papez 1937; Bubb et al. 2017; Aggleton 2010 |
| **Fornix** | Hippocampal fimbria (bilateral) | Mammillary bodies, septal nuclei, anterior thalamus, hypothalamus | Major hippocampal output. Carries theta rhythms and ACh. Left=verbal memory; Right=visuospatial memory | Fornix review (AJNR 2015) |
| **Uncinate fasciculus** | Temporal pole (BA38), anterior temporal lobe, amygdala | OFC (BA11/47), vmPFC, lateral prefrontal cortex | Top-down emotion regulation. Connects PFC to amygdala for inhibitory control. Reduced FA in depression/anxiety | Von Der Heide et al. 2013; Zuurbier et al. 2013 |
| **Cingulum bundle** | Subcallosal gyrus (frontal lobe) | Parahippocampal gyrus, uncus (temporal lobe), following cingulate gyrus | Connects ACC to PCC (default mode network core). Anterior=executive/attention; Temporal=episodic memory | Bubb et al. 2018; Jones et al. 2013 |
| **Arcuate fasciculus** | Posterior temporal cortex (Wernicke's area) | Inferior frontal gyrus (Broca's area) | Language pathway. Left AF=syntax; Right AF=prosody/emotional intonation processing | Catani et al. 2005; Catani & Thiebaut de Schotten 2008 |
| **Internal capsule (ALIC)** | Prefrontal cortex (vmPFC, vlPFC, dlPFC, dACC, dmPFC) | Thalamus (MD, anterior nuclei), brainstem | Prefrontal-thalamic relay. ALIC carries emotion/motivation/decision fibers. Target for DBS in OCD | Safadi et al. 2018 |
| **Stria terminalis** | Centromedial amygdala (CeA) | BNST, hypothalamus (VMH), septal area | Major amygdala output for sustained anxiety. CeA-CRH neurons -> BNST consolidate longer-lasting fear | Davis et al. 2010; Lebow & Chen 2016 |
| **Ventral amygdalofugal pathway** | Basolateral and central amygdala nuclei | Hypothalamus (lateral), septal nuclei, NAc, thalamus (MD), brainstem (PAG, raphe, LC, PBN), basal forebrain | Primary amygdala-to-brainstem pathway. Mediates fear/autonomic responses. 5 segments identified | Kamali et al. 2016; Pani et al. 2024 |
| **Anterior commissure** | Temporal pole, amygdala, inferior temporal gyrus (one hemisphere) | Contralateral temporal pole, amygdala, inferior temporal gyrus | Bilateral amygdala/temporal integration. Contralateral BLA-NAc aversive valence; ipsilateral=positive valence | Di Paola et al. 2020 |
| **Mammillothalamic tract** | Mammillary bodies | Anterior thalamic nuclei (AD, AV, AM) | Papez circuit link. Lesions produce anterograde amnesia | Aggleton 2010; Vann 2010 |

## 3. Neurotransmitter Projection Maps

| System | Origin | Target Regions | Receptors | Emotion Role |
|--------|--------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Dopamine (mesolimbic)** | VTA (A10) | NAc (shell/core), amygdala, hippocampus, septum, BNST | D1 (excitatory), D2 (inhibitory), D3 | Reward prediction error (Schultz 1997). "Wanting"/incentive salience (Berridge). Dorsomedial NAc shell=reward; ventromedial=salience |
| **Dopamine (mesocortical)** | VTA (A10) | PFC (mPFC, OFC, dlPFC), ACC | D1, D2, D4 | Cognitive control of emotion, working memory, decision-making |
| **Dopamine (nigrostriatal)** | SNc (A9) | Dorsal striatum (caudate, putamen) | D1, D2, D3, D5 | Motor control, habit formation, procedural learning |
| **Serotonin (5-HT)** | Dorsal raphe nucleus (B7) | Widespread: PFC, OFC, amygdala, hippocampus, hypothalamus, NAc, cingulate, insula | 5-HT1A (inhibitory), 5-HT2A (excitatory), 5-HT1B, 5-HT3, 5-HT4 | Mood regulation, anxiety modulation. Subpopulations project to reward- vs anxiety-related structures. SSRIs target this system |
| **Serotonin (median raphe)** | Median raphe nucleus (B8) | Hippocampus, septum, hypothalamus | 5-HT1A, 5-HT7 | Memory modulation, circadian rhythm, hippocampal theta |
| **Norepinephrine (NE)** | Locus coeruleus (A6) | Widespread: all cortex, thalamus, hypothalamus, amygdala, hippocampus, cerebellum, spinal cord | alpha-1 (excitatory), alpha-2 (inhibitory/autoreceptor), beta-1/2/3 | Arousal, vigilance, stress response. Phasic=exploitation; Tonic=exploration (Aston-Jones & Cohen 2005). GAIN modulation at NE-glutamate "hot spots" |
| **Acetylcholine (cortical)** | Basal forebrain: Nucleus basalis of Meynert (Ch4), medial septum (Ch1), diagonal band (Ch2/3) | Neocortex (widespread, topographic), amygdala, hippocampus | nAChR (nicotinic, ionotropic), mAChR M1-M5 (muscarinic, metabotropic) | Attention, cortical plasticity, arousal. Ch4 modulates PFC response to sensory input. Degenerated in Alzheimer's |
| **Acetylcholine (thalamic)** | Pedunculopontine (Ch5), laterodorsal tegmentum (Ch6) | Thalamus, basal ganglia, brainstem | nAChR, M1-M5 | Sleep-wake regulation, REM sleep, arousal modulation |
| **Oxytocin** | Paraventricular nucleus (PVN), supraoptic nucleus (SON) of hypothalamus | Amygdala (MeA, CeA), BNST, NAc, VTA, hippocampus, brainstem | OTR (Gq-coupled) | Social bonding, trust, anxiety reduction. PVN->MeA enhances social approach + anxiety; PVN->BNST suppresses anxiety. Inhibits CeA output via GABAergic interneurons -> reduces fear |
| **CRH** | PVN (hypothalamus), CeA (amygdala), BNST | PVN->median eminence->anterior pituitary (ACTH); CeA->BNST, brainstem, hypothalamus | CRH-R1 (anxiety), CRH-R2 (stress coping) | HPA axis initiation. CeA CRH neurons drive sustained fear/anxiety. Elevated CRH in depression and suicide |
| **Endogenous opioids (enkephalin, beta-endorphin, dynorphin)** | PAG (enkephalin), arcuate nucleus (beta-endorphin), NAc, amygdala, dorsal horn | PAG->raphe nuclei->spinal cord (descending pain modulation); NAc, amygdala, VTA, hypothalamus | mu (analgesia, reward), delta (mood), kappa (dysphoria, stress) | Pain suppression, reward/pleasure ("liking"). PAG opioids mediate stress-induced analgesia. PAG->raphe->spinal cord = descending pain inhibition. Mu-opioid in VTA disinhibits DA neurons |

## 8. Summary of Key Principles for Emotion Circuit Modeling

### Architecture
1. **No 1:1 emotion-region mapping** (Lindquist 2012, Pessoa 2008): Emotions emerge from dynamic coalitions of distributed networks, not dedicated modules
2. **Dual-system model** (Phillips 2003): Ventral system (fast, automatic: amygdala, insula, vSTR, vACC) + Dorsal system (slow, effortful: dlPFC, dACC, hippocampus)
3. **Triple network model** (Menon 2011): Salience network (AI+dACC) gates switching between DMN (self/internal) and CEN (executive/external)
4. **Valence encoding** (Janak & Tye 2015): BLA has distinct positive- and negative-valence neuron populations projecting to different CeA targets
5. **Predictive processing** (Barrett 2017): Brain constructs emotions as predictions from prior experience, not as reactions to stimuli

### Key Hubs
- **Amygdala (BLA+CeA)**: Valence detection, fear learning, emotional memory modulation
- **Anterior insula**: Interoception, awareness, salience detection (Von Economo neurons)
- **sgACC (BA25)**: Visceromotor, autonomic regulation, sadness/depression
- **dACC (BA24/32)**: Conflict monitoring, pain, cognitive-emotional integration
- **OFC**: Reward/punishment representation, value-based decision making
- **NAc**: Reward/motivation, incentive salience
- **PAG**: Defense behaviors, pain modulation, autonomic responses
- **Hypothalamus (PVN)**: HPA axis, oxytocin/CRH release, autonomic control

### Neuromodulatory Axes
- **DA (VTA->NAc/PFC)**: Reward prediction error, motivation, "wanting"
- **5-HT (DRN->widespread)**: Mood regulation, anxiety modulation (subpopulation-specific)
- **NE (LC->widespread)**: Arousal gain modulation, exploitation/exploration balance
- **ACh (NBM->cortex)**: Attention, cortical plasticity
- **OT (PVN->amygdala/BNST)**: Social bonding, fear reduction
- **CRH (PVN+CeA->HPA)**: Stress response, sustained anxiety
- **Opioids (PAG/NAc)**: Pain modulation, hedonic "liking"
